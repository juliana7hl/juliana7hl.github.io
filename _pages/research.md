## Research

I am a disease ecologist studying how environmental change, landscape structure, and human activities influence the transmission of vector-borne diseases.

### Dengue viral dynamics and spatial epidemiology

My current research integrates viral phylodynamics, urban ecology, and spatial epidemiology to understand how dengue virus lineages are introduced, spread, and persist in hyperendemic regions of Colombia.

### Landscape ecology of vector-borne diseases

My research in Panama examines how land-cover composition, fragmentation, topography, and environmental gradients influence the distribution of vectors and pathogen transmission.

### Community ecology of disease vectors

I study how environmental disturbance shapes sand fly communities, species co-occurrence, and the distribution of vectors involved in leishmaniasis transmission.
